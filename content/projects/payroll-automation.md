# Payroll Automation at Thriving Center of Psychology

**Company:** Thriving Center of Psychology
**Type:** Internal tooling / workflow automation
**Outcome:** $100K annual savings, thousands of invoicing hours eliminated within 6 months

## The Problem

TCP had a large and growing contractor network — therapists and providers working across multiple states. Payroll was being handled manually: invoices collected, reviewed, approved, and processed by hand. As the network scaled, this process wasn't keeping up. It was slow, error-prone, and consuming a disproportionate amount of operations time.

## What I Built

Designed and implemented an automated contractor payroll system using Rippling as the core platform. The solution created a bridge between provider scheduling and billing data and the payroll processing layer, eliminating the manual steps in between.

- Mapped the existing manual workflow and identified every step that could be automated
- Integrated Rippling with existing systems to automate invoice collection, approval routing, and payroll processing
- Built validation logic to catch errors before they reached the payment stage
- Reduced the invoicing cycle from days to hours
- Eliminated thousands of hours of manual work within the first 6 months

## Results

- $100,000 saved annually in operational costs
- Thousands of invoicing hours reclaimed within 6 months
- Error rate on contractor payments dropped significantly
- Operations team redirected time to higher-value work

## Why This Matters

This is a good example of the kind of internal tooling work that doesn't get talked about enough in product. The impact was immediate and measurable, and it freed up the team to focus on things that actually required human judgment. Automation doesn't have to be AI — sometimes it's just connecting systems that should have been connected already.
