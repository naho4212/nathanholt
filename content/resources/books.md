# Books I Recommend

## Product and Strategy

**The Mom Test** by Rob Fitzpatrick — The best book on customer interviews. Short, practical, and directly applicable. If you're doing user research, read this first.

**Inspired** by Marty Cagan — Good framework for how product teams should be structured and how PMs should think about their role. More relevant for growth-stage companies than early stage.

**Shape Up** by Ryan Singer (Basecamp) — A different way to think about product development cycles. Not prescriptive, but the ideas about appetite and shaping work are genuinely useful.

**Continuous Discovery Habits** by Teresa Torres — The best modern take on how to make discovery a regular practice rather than a one-time event.

## Business and Systems Thinking

**The Hard Thing About Hard Things** by Ben Horowitz — Honest about what leadership actually looks like in difficult situations. Not a framework, just real experience.

**High Output Management** by Andy Grove — Management fundamentals that hold up. The sections on leverage and meetings are worth the whole book.

**Thinking in Systems** by Donella Meadows — Changes how you see cause and effect in complex organizations. Relevant for anyone building platforms or working on operationally complex problems.

## AI and Technology

**The Pragmatic Programmer** by David Thomas and Andrew Hunt — Still useful even if you're not a full-time engineer. The mindset around craftsmanship and thinking about systems applies directly to product work.

## General

**Clear Thinking** by Shane Parrish — Mental models for better decisions. Practical and well-organized.
