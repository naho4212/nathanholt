# Common Questions

## Why are you looking?

My contract at Dosable wrapped up in late 2025. I've been working on personal projects, doing a targeted search, and being selective about what I join next. Actively looking for the right fit.

## What's your superpower?

Finding leverage. Whether that's automating a workflow that's eating 40% of the ops team's time, rebuilding a system that's become a bottleneck, or figuring out which feature will unlock the next growth curve — I'm good at identifying where the real constraint is and doing something about it. I don't separate strategy from execution.

## What kind of problems get you out of bed?

Operationally complex problems where the product and the business are deeply intertwined. Early-stage work where you're figuring out what to build, not just how to build it. AI and automation applied to real workflows, not demos. Internal tooling that makes teams dramatically more effective.

## What's your working style?

Direct and low-ego. I'd rather make a call and be wrong fast than spend two weeks building consensus around the obvious answer. I communicate up early when something is off-track and I don't pretend a brief is finished when it isn't. I think carefully about second-order effects and I get frustrated by decisions that optimize for optics over outcomes.

## Are you technical?

Yes, meaningfully so. Python, TypeScript, SQL, React — I can read and write all of them. Not a full-time engineer, but I use AI-native coding tools daily and I regularly take something from idea to working demo without help. The practical effect is I can have real conversations about tradeoffs without needing things translated for me.

## What does your ideal role look like?

End-to-end product ownership at a company where the problem is technically interesting or operationally complex. Close to engineering, close to customers, with visibility into the business. AI-first, data-intensive, or marketplace/platform companies are the sweet spot. Remote preferred, NYC hybrid open.

## What are you not looking for?

Pure execution roles, companies where product is downstream of sales or engineering with no real ownership, or roles that are primarily roadmap management without room to shape direction.

## What's your take on AI in product?

The most valuable use cases aren't the flashy ones. They're where someone is currently doing something repetitive and judgment-based at high volume with low cost of error. That's where you get real ROI. The hard part isn't the model — it's the data pipeline, the evaluation framework, and the product decisions about when to keep a human in the loop.

## Tell me about the payroll automation at TCP.

Thriving Center had a large contractor network — therapists and providers across multiple states. Payroll was handled manually and the process wasn't keeping up with growth. I designed and shipped an automated payroll system using Rippling that eliminated the manual steps in the invoicing and payment cycle. Result: $100K saved annually, thousands of invoicing hours reclaimed within 6 months, and the ops team's time redirected to higher-value work.
