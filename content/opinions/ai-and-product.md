# AI and Product

## Where I Actually Stand

Most AI product thinking right now is either too optimistic (AI will do everything) or too dismissive (it's just hype). The interesting work is in the middle — figuring out where LLMs create genuine leverage in real workflows and where they just add complexity.

## What I've Actually Shipped

At Thriving Center I built AI-assisted patient onboarding using Tellescope. The goal wasn't to impress anyone with AI — it was to cut the manual admin work that was slowing down time-to-first-session. Result: 40% reduction in admin work, 25% faster time-to-first-session. The AI was a means to an end, not the product.

PowSignal is entirely AI-native — built with Claude Code, Cursor, and OpenAI APIs. Building it has given me a ground-level view of what these tools actually do well and where they still need human judgment.

## What I Think About LLM Integration

The most valuable LLM use cases aren't the flashy ones. They're the places where someone is currently doing something repetitive and judgment-based — reviewing intake forms, routing support tickets, drafting first versions of structured outputs — where the cost of a mistake is low and the volume is high. That's where you get real ROI.

The hard part isn't the model. It's the data pipeline, the evaluation framework, and the product decisions about when to show the AI output versus when to keep a human in the loop.

## On Building with AI Tools

Working with Claude Code and Cursor daily has changed how I think about what a PM with technical fluency can ship. The gap between idea and working prototype is smaller than it's ever been. That's not a reason to skip engineering — it's a reason to think harder about where engineering time should go.

## On AI Hype

Most companies don't need a large language model. They need their data in one place, clean, with a simple way to act on it. Start there. The AI layer gets more powerful when the foundation is solid.
